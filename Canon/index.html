(Canon) cameras

My favorite film SLR was the Minolta SRT-101, because controls were as expected.
Super Takumar lenses were better, but Pentax usability was not there for me.

why Canon?

  • 20D was the first affordable DSLR with 8mp, IMO the minimum acceptable
  • a good range of lenses, with others easily adaptable to EF mount
  • 20D served until 7D II "solved" birds-in-flight challenges
    • Keeping autofocus point on target with a 400mm lens is tough without image stabilization.
      Adding a gunstock helps. In this case, actual (albeit hollow plastic for air rifle)


      Adding an LCD loupe and red dot finder, as on my Lumix FZ200:

      ... with Canon 7D II tweaked autofocus makes birds-in-flight image capture pretty easy.

  • APS-C seems also a sweet spot for photomicography
  • Canon APS-C 22.2x14.9mm sensor is a little smaller than others' 23.6x15.7mm,
    better filled by (e.g. Nikon CF) objectives' direct projection field (19-22mm).
  • thin flange adapters seem more available for Canon than other bodies
  • Magic Lantern software adds focus bracket to older Canon bodies
  • discontinued EOS M series bodies are available used at reduced prices, all >= 24mp,
    with mirrorless' smaller flange-to-sensor distance easier to replace e.g. C-mount adapters.
  • 90D and M6 mkII resolution and focus bracketing are nearly ideal, IMO.

Having fully intending to eventually get some generation of 5D, APS-C satisfied
until a 6D taught that image quality at lower light and wider angles were uncompelling (for my usages).

Canon acronyms

  • EOS = ElectoOptical System
  • EF = Electro-Focus (although some EF lenses are manual focus)
  • EF-S = Electro-Focus Small (APS-C)
  • EF-M / EOS-M = Electro-Focus Mirrorless (but only APS-C, e.g. M6 Mark II)
  • EOS R = Electro-Optical System Re-Imagined
  • RF = Re-Imagined Focus
    EF, EF-S, EF-M and RF all seemingly share lens control signalling;
    mounts differ mainly in diameters and sensor spacings,
    but Canon blocks OEMs using RF...?
